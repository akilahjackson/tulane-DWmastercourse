Who can I contact if I have technical questions?

For technical support, such as difficulty accessing any of the site pages, contact TRDirect@tulane.edu or call 504.988.1342. Our business hours are 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM ET, Monday–Friday.

Help

This course offers several ways to access the course content. You can work through the entire course in the order of presentation, target specific lessons where you may need more assistance, and use the course as a reference tool to find the specific information you need.

  • Use the course menu above the course content to locate a lesson or topic and jump directly to that content by clicking on the page link.
  • A breadcrumbs trail at the top left of the screen will take you to the first page of each lesson or topic. 
  • Navigation features at the bottom of the screen include back and next buttons to move from one page of the course to another. The first and last buttons will take you to the first and last pages of the course, respectively.
  • Use the Resources and Help accordion menus located below the course content to access additional support and information while taking the course. Course links typically open in a second window. When you have finished viewing the contents in the second window, close the window to return to the course.

To receive a Certificate of Completion for this course, you must complete the following:

Final Course Exam

After viewing the course content, you must complete a final course exam. A passing grade for the final exam is 70% or higher. You will be provided with unlimited attempts to pass the exam. If you did not successfully complete the exam, please review the course material and try again.

Required Course Survey

After completing the final course exam, you will complete a required survey. We will use your feedback to monitor and improve current and future course offerings.

Please see the Contacts tab for individual help not covered in the help files.

System Settings

This course is designed to work most effectively if your computer and internet connection meet certain minimal requirements. This course can be accessed using a Windows 10 PC or a Mac with High Sierra1, Mojave, or Catalina. Pop-up blockers should be disabled when viewing the course.

Internet Explorer 11 (for Windows 10), or the current version of Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, or Apple Safari (for Windows 10 and or Mac) is required. Many of our courses require Java and JavaScript enabled.

Links to External Websites

Links to websites outside this course will open in a new window or tab. Some browsers may minimize the course window. If this occurs, maximize the course window to return to the course.

Adobe Acrobat Reader

Adobe Acrobat Reader is required to access some documents in this course. If you need to download a free copy of Acrobat Reader, click here.

Internet Connection Speed

A minimum download speed of 1.5 Mbps is recommended for an optimal experience, which is commonly the speed associated with a basic DSL or a cellular/satellite connection. A faster connection, such as cable or fiber service, with further enhance your online experience. A Wi-Fi connection is generally acceptable, but it is dependent upon one of the two services mentioned above. You can check your internet connection speed at https://www.speedtest.net.

Bookmark/Resume Function

Some users of Internet Explorer may encounter inconsistent results using the Bookmark/Resume function that appears on the first screen in the course after initial access. If you experience problems using Internet Explorer, consider using Chrome, Firefox, or Safari as your browser.

What do I need to know about online courses in order to be successful with them?

Online learning is primarily self-directed. That means that the learners in the online program are responsible for accessing the course on a regular basis, for completing the readings and activities associated with the course without prompting by another person (such as an instructor). Information is primarily delivered over the computer with the learner setting the schedule.

How much time will a course take?

Participants can expect to spend 60-90 minutes per course in this curriculum.

Does this course require a textbook?

There are no textbook requirements for this course.

Are there any required course prerequisites?

No, prerequisites are required.
